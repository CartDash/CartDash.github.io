About the Game.

The game's purpose is to teach kids ages 4 to 8 how to follow the Canadian food guide.

Why is healthy eating important?

It's important for students to learn about this at an early age for them to be aware of their own nutrition. Studies have proven that students who are healthy are more energetic and willing to participate in class compared to students lacking nutrients. Cart Dash introduces your students to Canada's food guide in a fun and friendly environment. Children will also be able to see their classes leaderbaord to encourage learning in a friendly competitive environment.

Resources

To view the full food guide please visit: https://www.canada.ca/en/health-canada/services/canada-food-guides.html

Levels 1-4

Levels 1 through 4 introduce young children (ages 4-8) the 4 different food categories: fruits and vegetables, grain products, milk and alternatives, and meats and alternatives. They will be able to pick out which foods are in these categories and will learn how many servings in one day they need of each. In these levels they will also have to watch out for chocolate Running Chocobar Man and Cookie Man!

Levels 5-10

Once levels 1 through 4 have been completed the following levels 5 through 10, mix all the food categories together. This allows the children to revise all the categories again and pick out the right amount of foods from each category all in the same game level. The obsticles in the game will become increasingly difficult in these levels.

  • Level 1 - Fruits and Vegetables

    This level introduces children to what fruits and vegetables are. Daily, they should enjoy 5 servings from this category. Some examples they will be shown from this category are: kale, carrots, bananas, grapes, apples, and tomatoes.

  • Level 2 - Grain Products

    This level introduces children to what grain products are. Daily, they should enjoy 4 servings from this category. Some examples they will be shown from this category are: bread, quinoa, rice, cereal, and pasta.

  • Level 3 - Milk and Alternatives

    This level introduces children to what milk and alternatives are. Everyday they should consume 2 servings from this category. Some examples displayed in this level include: milk, yogurt, kefir, cheese, and soy milk.

  • Level 4 - Meats and Alternatives

    This level introduces children to what meats and alternatives to meats are. They will learn that everyday they should have 1 serving from this food group. Some examples of this food group are seafood, legumes, tofu, eggs, and nuts.
