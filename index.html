About the Game.

The game's purpose is to teach kids ages 4 to 8 how to follow the Canadian food guide.

Why is healthy Eating important?

It's important for students to learn about this at an early age for them to be aware of their own nutrition. Studies have proven that students who are healthy are more energetic and willing to participate in class compared to students lacking nutrients.

Resources

To view the full food guide please visit: https://www.canada.ca/en/health-canada/services/canada-food-guides.html

Levels 1-4

Levels 1 through 4 introduce young children to the 4 different 4 categories in the foodguide as well as how much they need to consume.

Levels 5-10

Once levels 1 through 4 have been completed the following levels combined all the learned information into a more fun and challenging experience.

  • Level 1 - Fruits and Vegetables

    This level introduces children to what fruits and vegetables are. Daily, they should enjoy 5 servings from this category. Some examples they will be shown from this category are: kale, carrots, bananas, grapes, apples, and tomatoes.

  • Level 2 - Grain Products

    This level introduces children to what grain products are. Daily, they should enjoy 4 servings from this category. Some examples they will be shown from this category are: bread, quinoa, rice, cereal, and pasta.

  • Level 3 - Milk and Alternatives

    This level introduces children to what milk and alternatives are. Everyday they should consume 2 servings from this category. Some examples displayed in this level include: milk, yogurt, kefir, cheese, and soy milk.

  • Level 4 - Meats and Alternatives

    This level introduces children to what meats and alternatives to meats are. They will learn that everyday they should have 1 serving from this food group. Some examples of this food group are seafood, legumes, tofu, eggs, and nuts.
